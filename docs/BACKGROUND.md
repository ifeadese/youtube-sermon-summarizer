# Context & Background

Over a year ago, I started a blog section on my church's official website. The blog was intended to improve our SEO, and also help as an alternative way to recap/catch-up on previous teachings in much less time than having to watch or listen to the whole thing on a media streaming platform. The process for generating content for the blog was:

- Get the URL from the youtube video for the sunday sermon
- Obtain the transcript from https://kome.ai/tools/youtube-transcript-generator.
- Feed the transcript into ChatGPT and interact with it to produce a well-written, unmanipulated article
- Post it on the church blog via Squarespace

Sometimes kome.ai would not be able to generate the transcript for either technical reasons or because my device / identity has hit a rate limit that triggers a pay wall to a paid subscription. In those cases, I am forced to explore another free online tool like NoteGPT that allows me extract a non-numbered transcript in the exactly I prefer it to be. ChatGPT was also not so consistent in the quality of outputs it gave me. I tried maintaining a chat thread, a context-shared project folder and a dedicated GPT, but I was not experiencing the quickness in process/procedure I wanted.

I was able to do this weekly for sometime but after a while, the time taken to do this could range anywhere from minutes to hours. depending on the quality of the LLMs output etc. I want to design a product. Very simple MVP first. That aims to solve my problem there. Something cost effective, easily maintainable, reliable and performant and with great UX and an impeccable minimalistic UI. Hint: I want to build it like an engineering product with a vision of it being useful to many churches in the long term. but first I want to build only what is necessary and only build more as demand or evidence of need becomes clear. Like a pilot-style development.
